ENIAC: The First Computer

ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer) was one of the first general-purpose electronic digital computers. Completed in 1945, it marked a significant milestone in the history of computing.

Key Facts About ENIAC:

  1. ENIAC was designed by John W. Mauchly and J. Presper Eckert.
  2. It weighed about 30 tons and filled an entire room.
  3. ENIAC could perform thousands of calculations per second, which was revolutionary at the time.

ENIAC's Impact:

ENIAC was not only the first computer of its kind, but it also paved the way for future advancements in computing technology.

ENIAC's Legacy

The ENIAC set the stage for future developments, eventually leading to the creation of personal computers, laptops, and other modern-day computing devices.
